About me:

Welcome to my personal landing page in the web!
My name is Martin Jung and I am an aspiring next-generation ecologist.

I am a PhD researcher in Environmental Science at the University of Sussex, United Kingdom. In my PhD I will move to a global point of view and continue investigating the influence of anthropogenic drivers, such as land-use, on biodiversity across space and time. Specifically I will analyse large spatial-temporal remote-sensing products and combine this with the best biodiversity models currently available.

I did my Bachelors at the University of Marburg, Germany, in which I investigated the response of temperate plant-pollinator networks towards forest fragmentation. For my thesis I conducted over three months of fieldwork in one of the last patches of remaining "primary" forest in Europe, the Białowieża National Park, Poland.

Afterwards I moved to Denmark, where I wrote my MSc thesis at the Center for Macroecology, Evolution and Climate investigating the congruence between a broad-scale biodiversity model and independent field data. For my thesis I combined a meta-analytical approach and on-the-ground field work, where I sampled bird communities across a band of different land-use intensities for two and a half months in the Taita hills, Kenya and Mount Kilimanjaro, Tanzania.

I am broadly interested in Ecology and Conservation and have a personal taxonomic background in ornithology and entomology. My current research is focussed on the interplay between macroecology and natural resource management in the face of global change.
